KPK

The Capital of Khyber Pakhtunkhwa is Peshawar while its major cities include Mardan, Mingora, Kohat, and Abbottabad.


Mardan:

Takht-i-Bahi (The Throne of Bahi) is one of the most well preserved Budhist monasteries in Pakistan. Located 17 km from Mardan on hill, this monastery was founded in 1st century and remained an important Budhist educational and cultural center for many centuries. Many of the rooms and buildings inside the monastery are still quite well preserved though mosto f the relics and statues have been taken away over a period of time. It's a full day visit if you are going from Islamabad but monastery itself is a couple of hours event.


Local Tip: Be sure to try some of the local dishes, such as Chapli Kebabs, Seekh Kebabs, and traditional Pashtun bread


Swat:

It has very historical artefacts, some of them are thousands of years old. There were some Buddha Statue and some of the artefacts of their religion; one thing that is most noticeable is the footprint which is 3 times bigger than the size of an average human, it is said that the Buddhist had firm belief that these are the footprints of Buddha and model of temple made of rock. There is also some clothes and accessories of Swat customs. The traditional pure wood furniture is also displayed there.


Local Tip: When visiting Swat, it's important to respect the local customs and traditions. Swat is a conservative area, and it's advisable to dress modestly, especially when visiting religious sites.


Kohat:

The best part of Khyber Pass is yet to come. The military there is developing a series of museums at the colonial area mountain posts of Ali Masjid, Landikotal and Michni. It gets hot in summers, try getting there in Spring time or winters.


Local Tip: Visit the Kohat Fort, also known as Sum Temple, is a historical site dating back to the Sikh era. It's a great place to explore the architecture and learn about the region's history.


Punjab

The Capital of the province is Lahore and its largest city is Faisalabad. Districts include Gujranwala, Bahawalpur, Mianwali, Jhang, Khanewal, Shikarpur, Narowal, Hafizabad, Rawalpindi, Multan, Ahmadpur.


Faisalabad:

Apart from industrial and agricultural aspect, this city has some important historical sites. Faisalabad Clock Tower is one of the oldest historical sites that is from the time of British Raj standing in its original state. During the nineteenth century, British ruled much of the South Asia and they built so many sites here and many of them are not in the original state and are ruined with the passage of time. However, one of the luckiest sites is the clock tower of Faisalabad that exists in its original condition. British built this Clock Tower.


Local Tip: Enjoy the local street food, shop for fabrics, and take in the vibrant atmosphere around Ghanta Ghar. It's an excellent way to connect with the heart of Faisalabad and experience its unique charm.


Rawalpindi:

Raja Bazaar is the main commercial, residential hub and shopping district and a union council of Rawalpindi. It is located near to the Fawara Chowk. Raja Bazaar Rawalpindi has some major business and commercial centres, branches of major banks and wide residential areas of British colonial era.


Local Tip: Haggling is a common practice in Raja Bazaar. Vendors often expect customers to negotiate prices, so don't be afraid to bargain for a better deal.


Lahore:

The Badshahi Mosque is an iconic Mughal-era congregational mosque in Lahore, capital of the Pakistani province of Punjab. The mosque is located opposite of Lahore Fort in the outskirts of the Walled City and is widely considered to be one of Lahore's most iconic landmarks.


Local Tip: Don't forget to enjoy a hot cup of doodh patti (milky tea) at a local dhaba or tea stall. It's a staple drink for many Lahoris.


Sindh

The capital of Sindh is Karachi while its major cities include Hyderabad (the second-largest city in Sindh), Sukkur, Larkana, Nawabshah, Sehwan, Shikarpur, Mirpurkhas, and Thatta. Sindh is known for its beautiful beaches along with the Arabian tribal culture.


Karachi:

Port Grand is one of the finest developments that celebrates the city of Karachi with diverse concepts in food, art, leisure, entertainment, adventure, fun and shopping, A cultural hub on Pakistan’s Seaport, celebrations at Port Grand are a truly delightful experience!


Local Tip: Don't miss trying out "Bun Kebab" - a local burger made with a spicy patty served in a bun, as well as "Golgappa" or "Pani Puri," which are hollow, crispy shells filled with tangy and spicy water and various fillings.


Hyderabad:

The Tombs of Talpur Mirs are a complex of tombs of the ruling Talpur Mirs of Sindh who reigned from 1784 to 1843. The tombs are also known as Cubbas. These tombs are located in Hirabad, Hyderabad in the Sindh province of Pakistan.


Local Tip: Don't miss out on tandoori kebabs and grilled meats. You can find stalls and small eateries that serve succulent tandoori dishes that are sure to tantalize your taste buds.


Multan:

The City Wall of Multan or Faseel e Multan is an ancient wall encircling the old city of Multan. It was built and rebuilt many times as it was destroyed in battles. The original construction time is unknown but it was present during the Siege of Multan and damaged badly. Part of this wall near Haram Gate was destroyed and was used by British troops to enter into the city.


Local Tip: Look for local handicrafts like blue pottery, camel skin products, and exquisite handwoven textiles. You can find these in the bazaars at reasonable prices.

Balochistan

Quetta is the capital of Balochistan. It is the largest province in Pakistan, known for its vast and rugged landscapes, rich culture, and unique history. Balochistan offers a unique travel experience for adventurers and those interested in exploring off-the-beaten-path destinations. Its major cities include Turbat, Khuzdar, Chaman and Gwadar.


Quetta:

Hanna Lake is one of the most visited and accessible lakes in Balochistan. There is a lakeside restaurant with picnic tables shaded by pine trees at the end of the river, where families can enjoy the food and weather. On the eastern side of the lake stands the Hayat Durrani Water Sports Academy (HDWSA), the first and only rowing, canoeing, kayaking and sailing training and championships organising centre in Balochistan Province, with provision of rough swimming facility.


Local Tip: Try the delicious local cuisine. Quetta is known for its unique and flavorful dishes, including Sajji and Rosh. Sajji is a traditional Balochi dish made with marinated skewered lamb or chicken, which is roasted over an open flame for a distinct smoky flavor. Rosh is a hearty and spicy mutton or beef soup that's perfect for warming up during the cooler Quetta evenings.


Khuzdar:

Hingol National Park or Hungol National Park is the one of the largest national parks in Pakistan, located in the Makran coastal region. The park covers an area of about 6,100 square kilometres (2,400 sq mi) and is located 190 km from Karachi in the three districts of Gwadar, Lasbela and Awaran in Balochistan. Hingol was declared a national park in 1988. The park is named after the southern part of the Hangul River, which flows along the shores of the Arabian Sea and is home to large numbers of waterfowl and a wealth of marine life.


Local Tip: Khuzdar has a number of historical sites, including the famous Khuzdar Fort. This 18th-century fort is an architectural marvel and offers a glimpse into the area's history. There are also prehistoric sites and rock engravings in the region, so if you're interested in archaeology, you might want to explore these.


Gwadar:

The Gwadar Port is situated on the Arabian Sea at Gwadar in Balochistan province of Pakistan and is under the administrative control of the Maritime Secretary of Pakistan and operational control of the China Overseas Port Holding Company. The port features prominently in the China–Pakistan Economic Corridor (CPEC) plan, and is considered to be a link between the Belt and Road Initiative and the Maritime Silk Road projects. It is about 120 kilometres (75 mi) southwest of Turbat, and 170 kilometres (110 mi) to the east of Chabahar Port (Sistan and Balochistan Province in Iran).


Local Tip: Don't miss the opportunity to savor the catch of the day, which may include fish, prawns, crabs, and other seafood delicacies. Enjoying a seafood meal by the Arabian Sea while watching the sunset is a unique and memorable experience in Gwadar.

Gilgit Baltistan

A breathtakingly beautiful region located in the northernmost part of Pakistan. It is renowned for its stunning landscapes, including majestic mountains, pristine rivers, and picturesque valleys. Gilgit-Baltistan is situated in the northern part of Pakistan, sharing borders with India, China, and Afghanistan. The region is home to some of the world's highest peaks, including K2, the second-highest mountain on Earth.


The Karakoram Highway:

The Karakoram Highway, also known as the Friendship Highway in China, was built by the governments of Pakistan and China. It was started in 1962 and was completed and opened to the public in 1978. Pakistan initially favored routing through Mintaka Pass. In 1966, China, citing the fact that Mintaka would be more susceptible to air strikes, recommended the steeper Khunjerab Pass instead.[6] About 810 Pakistanis and about 200 Chinese workers died,[7] mostly in landslides and falls, while building the highway. Over 140 Chinese workers who died during the construction are buried in the Chinese cemetery in Gilgit.[8] The route of the KKH traces one of the many paths of the ancient Silk Road.


Local Tip: Be prepared for unpredictable weather conditions, especially if you plan to visit during the winter months. The highway passes through some extremely high-altitude areas and mountain passes, and weather can change rapidly. It's essential to check the weather forecast before starting your journey and to carry warm clothing, blankets, and extra supplies in case you get stuck due to bad weather or road closures.


Hunza Valley:

Hunza was formerly a princely state bordering Xinjiang (autonomous region of China) to the northeast and Pamir to the northwest, which survived until 1974, when it was finally dissolved by Zulfikar Ali Bhutto. The state bordered the Gilgit Agency to the south and the former princely state of Nagar to the east. The state capital was the town of Baltit (also known as Karimabad); another old settlement is Ganish Village which means 'Baba Ganesh village' (a Buddhist name).


Local Tip: Try the fresh, organic fruits and dried apricots that the Hunza Valley is famous for. The region's fertile soil and natural farming methods result in some of the most flavorful and nutritious fruits you'll ever taste. Also try the local cuisine, particularly the traditional dish called "Chapshuro." Chapshuro is a delicious and hearty meal made by stuffing minced meat (usually beef or yak) and various spices into a pocket of unleavened bread.


Hushe Valley:

Hushe Valley is a valley in Ghanche District, Gilgit-Baltistan, Pakistan. Formed by the Hushe River, the valley runs from Masherbrum Mountain (at 7,821 meters) and nearby glaciers south to the Shyok River, opposite Khaplu. Hushe village lies in the center of the valley. The valley is known for the surrounding high mountains of the Karakoram range, including several peaks above six thousands meters like Laila Peak, K6, and K7. Murtaza peak is 5100m just behind K6 base camp and is considered epicenter of trackers and beginners. There are many sub-valleys in the area, including Gondogor, Charkusa, Nagma, Nanbroq and Mashabrum.


Local Tip: If you plan to trek to higher altitudes, it's essential to acclimatize properly. Spend a few days at lower altitudes to allow your body to adjust to the thinner air, reducing the risk of altitude sickness.

Azad Jammu & Kashmir

A beautiful and unique region located in the northern part of Pakistan. It is often referred to as "AJK" and is known for its stunning natural beauty, diverse landscapes, and rich cultural heritage.


Muzzafarabad:

Muzaffarabad is city in Pakistani-administered Azad Kashmir in the disputed Kashmir region. It is the largest city and the capital of Azad Kashmir, which is a Pakistani-administered administrative territory. The city is located in Muzaffarabad District, near the confluence of the Jhelum and Neelum rivers. The district is bounded by the Pakistani province of Khyber Pakhtunkhwa in the west, the Kupwara and Baramulla districts of Indian-administered Jammu and Kashmir in the east, and the Neelum District in the north.


Local Tip: explore the Chinar Bagh (Chinar Garden) during the autumn season. This beautiful garden is famous for its stunning Chinar trees, which turn vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall.


Amarnath Cave:

The cave is a holy place for Hindu pilgrims, because of its ice content. The ice pillar present inside the cave is also called ice lingham, the phallic symbol of Lord Shiva. Two smaller ice stalagmite represent Parvati, his wife, and Ganesha, his son. It is a 150 feet high and 90 feet long cave located nearly 145 km east of Srinagar. Located in a Himalayan glacial valley at a height of approximately 4,000m the cave has four ice formations, which are considered figurines of different Hindu gods.


Local Tip: it's important to check the current status of the pilgrimage and any travel advisories, as the region's security situation can change.


Gulmarg Gandola

Known to be the second-highest and the second-longest cable car in the world, it attracts thousands of tourists and thrillseekers throughout the year. It is a two-stage gondola lift that has the potential to ferry approximately 600 individuals per hour from and to Kongdoori Mountain.


Local Tip: Arrive early in the morning to avoid long lines and crowds.
